[Always-on policy](./always-on-policy) pins the non-negotiable truths. Everything else — password resets, billing, integrations — belongs in **reference articles** your agent pulls when a question needs them. In Arivie these are the same Markdown files, with one different frontmatter field: `usage_mode: auto`.

## An FAQ page

Drop files under `context/`. Each is a knowledge page with a short summary and tags for discovery:

```md title="context/faq-password.md"
---
summary: How a user resets their Aurora password.
usage_mode: auto
tags: [account, password, login]
---
# Resetting your password

1. Go to **aurora.example/login** and click **"Forgot password?"**.
2. Enter the email on your Aurora account and submit.
3. Check your inbox for a reset link (it expires in **60 minutes**).
4. Click the link, choose a new password (min 12 characters), and sign in.

If the email never arrives, check spam, then confirm the address is the one tied to your workspace. SSO users cannot reset a password here — they sign in through their identity provider.
```

Billing works the same way — declarative facts, not a procedure:

```md title="context/faq-billing.md"
---
summary: Where to change billing, plans, and payment methods in Aurora.
usage_mode: auto
tags: [billing, plans, invoices]
---
# Billing and plans

- **Change plan:** Workspace owners go to **Settings → Billing → Change plan**. Upgrades are immediate and prorated; downgrades apply at the next renewal.
- **Update card:** **Settings → Billing → Payment method**.
- **Invoices:** **Settings → Billing → Invoices** — download any past invoice as PDF.
- **Seats:** adding a teammate adds a seat to the next invoice; removing one credits the proration.

Only a **workspace owner or billing admin** can see the Billing tab.
```

## Frontmatter that matters

| Field | Role |
|---|---|
| `summary` | One-line description — used when listing and ranking pages |
| `tags` | Loose categories (`billing`, `account`, …) for humans and retrieval |
| `usage_mode: auto` | Page is **not** injected every turn; the agent retrieves it when relevant |

Add as many pages as you need — `faq-integrations.md`, `faq-sso.md`, `faq-export.md`, whatever your product surface requires. Commit them; your team's normal doc review is the governance loop. Context is *what's true*; if you need a *how to run the weekly audit* runbook, that is a skill, not a context page.

## Not in the prompt — until they are

Unlike `always` policy, these bodies never land in the instructions wholesale. On startup Arivie loads them from `context.root`; at question time the agent decides whether to search. Ask *"How do I reset my password?"* and the model calls `search_context`, gets the password FAQ back, and quotes the steps — Forgot password link, 60-minute expiry, the works. Ask about refunds and the always-injected policy still wins without a search.

That retrieval step is configured once in `arivie.config.ts`. Next: [how `auto` pages become searchable](./retrieval).
